The Omora Ethnobotanical Park is an interdisciplinary research and education center for the Cape Horn Biosphere Reserve. This reserve protects the marine and terrestrial ecosystems of the southern end of the Americas. Its extends from the Almirantazgo Sound in the northwest through the Darwin Mountain Range to the Beagle Channel and Navarino Island in the east, and Hoste Island and the Cape Horn Archipelago in the south. This reserve preserves habitats for more than 1000 species of liverworts, mosses, and lichens. At least 50% of these species are endemic to the temperate and sub-Antarctic regions of southwestern South America!
